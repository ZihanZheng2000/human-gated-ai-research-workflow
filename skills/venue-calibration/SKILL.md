# Venue Calibration Skill

## Use When

- the user names a target journal, conference, or outlet
- the user is unsure where the work should go
- venue expectations may affect novelty, methods, length, citations, figures, or disclosures
- similar articles from the target venue should shape data scale, validation depth, article type, structure, figures, or citation density

## Inputs

- user need or tentative research topic
- domain or audience
- intended output type
- time and rigor constraints

## Procedure

1. Ask whether the user has a target venue or output route.
2. If not, recommend 2-3 plausible routes and explain tradeoffs.
3. If a specific venue is chosen, retrieve official author instructions when needed.
4. Summarize scope, article type, structure, word limits, citation style, figure/table rules, data/code requirements, and disclosure requirements.
5. For formal manuscript routes, inspect 2-5 recent structurally similar venue exemplars when possible.
6. Extract exemplar patterns: section structure, evidence depth, methods detail, figure/table count and style, citation density, Discussion/limitations pattern, and disclosure/data statements.
7. Translate official rules and exemplar patterns into constraints for Planning, Modeling, or Reporting.
8. Record venue/output status as confirmed, system-recommended, provisional, undecided, or deferred.

## Outputs

- venue/output calibration table
- official venue requirements table
- venue exemplar scan table
- venue-derived plan constraints
- target audience and output-format notes
- constraints that affect Planning, Modeling, or Reporting

## Guardrails

- Do not force a venue too early.
- Do not use non-official venue rules when official instructions are available.
- Do not treat exemplar patterns as hard rules; use them as evidence of venue conventions.
- If venue demands stronger evidence than the project can support, flag the mismatch.
